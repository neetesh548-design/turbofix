What really happens in those "5 minutes"

Every unrecorded breakdown is a mystery your team solves from scratch next time it happens. A machine stops. The worker shouts, or walks to find someone. Here's the real timeline:

Without TurboFix
0:00 — Machine stops

Worker notices the problem. Production on this machine halts. The clock starts.

0:00 – 3:00 — Finding supervisor

Worker leaves the station. Walks to supervisor's last known location. Supervisor may be handling another issue, in a meeting, or on a break.

3:00 – 8:00 — Supervisor walks to machine

Supervisor drops their current task (another cost). Walks to the machine. Asks the worker to explain verbally — details get lost.

8:00 – 15:00 — Finding technician

Supervisor now walks to find the maintenance technician. Repeats the explanation. Details degrade with each retelling. Technician walks to the machine.

15:00 – 20:00 — Diagnosis begins

Technician finally sees the machine. Asks the worker again: "What happened?" Twenty minutes have passed. No record exists.

Time lost:20+ min
People interrupted:3-5x
Records created:0
Knowledge lost:100%
With TurboFix

Same supervisor, 10x faster.

0:00 — Worker sends a WhatsApp

Scans the machine's QR code, describes the issue in their own language. Done in 30 seconds, without leaving their station.

0:01 — Everyone is notified instantly

Supervisor, technician, and owner all receive a WhatsApp alert with AI-translated summary, urgency level, and machine location. Simultaneously.

0:02 — Supervisor acknowledges

Supervisor replies "ACK T001 10min" from wherever they are. Worker gets instant confirmation: "Help is on the way — ETA 10 minutes."

0:10 — Technician arrives prepared

Technician read the AI summary, saw the photo, and already knows what tools to bring. No game of telephone. No wasted trip.

Time to notify:1 min
